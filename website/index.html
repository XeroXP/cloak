A library to bootstrap the use of Dagger in your Android app

Introduction

Dependency injection allows the removal of hard-coded dependencies and makes it possible to change them, whether at run-time or compile-time.

Traditionally Guice has been the go-to library for dependency injection, with RoboGuice bringing this functionality to Android. Dagger is a new dependency injector specfically optimized for Android and interactive Java apps. In particular, it reduces the overhead required by Guice at startup.

This library eliminates some of the boilerplate required to wire your Dagger app. For a minimal working app, all that is required is to switch your imports to the corresponding classes provided, and provide a module listing all your @Inject points. To see a working sample, look in the cloak-sample folder at the root of the project.

This library also sets up for "view injection" with ButterKnife so you never have to call findViewById again.

Usage

Simply switch your imports so that your Application class extends from CloakedApplication, your activities extend from CloakedActivity and fragments extend from CloakedFragment. Your application class must provide a module to the applicationGraph listing all injection points for your app.

To see a working sample, look in the cloak-sample folder at the root of the project.
